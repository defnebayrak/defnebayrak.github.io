Why did we do this project?

  • As the damage to living beings increases day by day, we want to show the cruelty that predatory creatures also suffer.

Project Goal & Societal Benefits

  • Our main goal on this project is to create awareness about animal cruelty with hunting, and we want to show the effects of hunting, which is seen as a means of entertainment in society, on living things.

Introduction of data & problem statement

We got our data from the RSPB open data source site.

Source

Our data includes confirmed incidents of raptors that took place in the UK. The dataset consists of 1297 rows and 13 columns.

Distribution of incident types by country

  • We see that almost 50% of the damage done to birds is shot. The distribution of their nests is also one of the least common events in the data. We can say that these events are most common in England.

Distribution and number of incident types by years

  • From 2007 to 2019, we can say that the lowest incidents were in 2019, even if we did not observe a constant decrease. Every year we see poisoning or being shot head-on.

Distribution of incidents by seasons

  • If we make an evaluation according to the seasons, we can say that in the spring time more incidents occur twice than in other seasons.

5 Counties with the highest incidents

  • Angus, Highland, North Yorkshire, Perth and Kinross and Scottish Borders are one of the counties where the incidents took place the most.

Raptor most exposed to incidents

  • Buzzards, other birds of prey and red kites are among the most exposed creatures.
